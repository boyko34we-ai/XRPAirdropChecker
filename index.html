Trust lines

Opt in to
receive tokens.

XRPL requires a manual Trust Line before you can receive any token. Unlike Ethereum, tokens can't be "pushed" to your address — you must explicitly authorize each token issuer.

1
Open XUMM (Xaman)
Open your XUMM or Crossmark wallet. Navigate to the Tokens section. This is where you manage Trust Lines for your XRPL account.
XUMM · CROSSMARK · LEDGER XRPL APP
2
Find the official issuer address
Always copy the token issuer address from the official project website or verified social accounts only. Never trust addresses from Telegram, DMs, or unofficial sources. Verify on XRPL explorers like Bithomp or XRPScan.
OFFICIAL ONLY · VERIFY ON XRPSCAN · NEVER DMs
3
Set Trust Line in XUMM
In XUMM, go to Add Token → enter the issuer address and currency code (e.g. NIGHT, FLR, EVRS). Setting a Trust Line costs ~2 XRP reserve — this is not a fee, it's locked as long as the Trust Line exists and returned when you remove it.
~2 XRP RESERVE · RETURNED ON REMOVAL
4
Tokens can now be received
Once the Trust Line is set, the project can deliver tokens to your address during the airdrop distribution. Always set Trust Lines before the distribution date — not after. Check official project channels for exact timing.
SET BEFORE DISTRIBUTION · CHECK OFFICIAL TIMELINE
Trust Line reserve
~2 XRP per line
Each Trust Line on XRPL locks ~2 XRP as a reserve — not a fee, it's returned when you remove the Trust Line. If your wallet drops below the minimum reserve (10 XRP base + 2 XRP per Trust Line), you may not be able to transact.
Why XRPL requires opt-in
Spam prevention
XRPL's Trust Line requirement prevents token spam — no one can fill your wallet with worthless tokens without your consent. This is fundamentally different from Ethereum where anyone can send tokens to any address. It's a security feature, not a limitation.
Required Trust Lines for tracked drops
NIGHT, FLR, EVRS
Set Trust Lines before distribution dates for: NIGHT (Midnight network issuer), FLR (Flare Network), SGB (Songbird), EVRS (Evernode), RLUSD (Ripple). XUMM supports all of these natively.

Stay safe

Airdrop scams
are everywhere.

Every major airdrop attracts scammers. Know the rules before you claim anything.

🔑
Never share your seed phrase

Legitimate airdrops never require your 12 or 24 word seed phrase, private key, or secret key. Any website, bot, or person asking for this is a scam — regardless of how official it looks. Your seed phrase is the only thing that grants access to your wallet.

✗ Never enter seed phrase on ANY website
🌐
Use official URLs only

Always verify you're on the official project website before clicking claim. Scammers create lookalike domains (midnight-claim.com, flare-airdrop.io) that steal funds when you connect. Bookmark official sites. Check domain age and official social media for links.

✓ Verify domain via official Twitter/X account
💬
Ignore DMs about airdrops

Scammers flood Telegram, Discord, and X with DMs claiming you have unclaimed tokens. No legitimate project will DM you to claim an airdrop. All real airdrop announcements come from verified official accounts and are widely covered in crypto media.

✗ Never click airdrop links from DMs
🏦
Self-custody required

Most XRPL airdrops require XRP in a self-custody wallet (XUMM, Crossmark, Ledger) — not on an exchange. Exchange-held XRP means the exchange controls the actual XRPL address. Move to self-custody before the next snapshot to ensure eligibility.

✓ Use XUMM or Ledger for best coverage
🔍
Verify on XRPL explorers

Before setting a Trust Line or interacting with any airdrop contract, verify the issuer address on Bithomp.com or XRPScan.com. Check transaction history, account age, and token details. Scam tokens often have no history or suspicious activity.

✓ Check bithomp.com or xrpscan.com first
⏰
Claim windows expire

Most airdrops have a claim window — typically 60 to 90 days. After the window closes, unclaimed tokens may be burned or returned to the treasury. The NIGHT token claim window was 60 days (Aug–Oct 2025). Check deadlines and act before expiry.
